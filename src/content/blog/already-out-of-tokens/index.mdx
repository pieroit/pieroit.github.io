So you've burned through your AI tokens again, and now you're sitting there
waiting for them to unlock. That whole dance — the rate limits, the waiting, the
paying more to wait less — is a big part of why everyone is drifting toward
Chinese models. Everyone except you, of course.

## The numbers are moving fast

Let me show you a chart. This one comes from OpenRouter, an aggregator that
gives you access to both Western and Asian models through their APIs — so you
pay purely per usage.

Last year, non-American models accounted for about **10%** of usage on
OpenRouter. This year that figure has climbed to **40%**.

And then there's the price. Compared to the top of the range — Anthropic's
models — the Chinese models in the same tier cost a quarter of the price. Half
of half.

The usual objection is privacy: "I trust the Americans more." Do you, though? At
this point you've already handed all your data to the Americans, so you might as
well give it to the Chinese too and let them fight it out. What do you care?

## What this actually means in practice

The Chinese open models — whether you use them through an API or run them
locally — enable a strategy the closed vendors don't.

- **Start on the cloud.** You're up and running immediately, and it costs very
  little.
- **Move in-house later if you want.** Because these models are open, you can
  eventually bring them home with a local setup. That comes with its own
  headaches, but the _option_ exists.

That's the key point: closed vendors like OpenAI and Anthropic simply don't let
you make this move. With open models you keep the exit door open.

## The catch: performance

People say the high-end Chinese models are on par with the Western ones. That's
not really true — especially compared to Anthropic.

Here's a concrete example. The subtitles you see in my videos are generated
through a set of **skills**, and inside those skills are scripts that do a whole
chain of things. Setting that up with Claude was immediate. It barely made any
mistakes, and it worked the moment I stood it up.

Doing the exact same thing with the high-end Chinese models — I tried both a
top-tier one and GLM — I had to rewrite all the skills and all the scripts, make
them far more explicit, far easier to follow, so the underlying language model
could actually get the work done.

## So what do you choose?

It comes down to two postures:

- **Pay more, stay lazy.** Sit back on the couch, belly out, and let the premium
  model handle everything.
- **Pay less, roll up your sleeves.** Use the Chinese models, save a fortune, but
  do the extra work to make them behave.
