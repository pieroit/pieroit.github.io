So there's this whole thing from **[Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com)** again: "let's slow down with AI, because from now on the software basically writes itself." It all sounds lovely, and it's all technically true - but let me add my own footnote, since I've apparently become the resident bird of ill omen. Consider this a little extra note on top of what Italy's very talented AI influencers have already said.

## The part nobody says out loud

One of the fundamental reasons Anthropic keeps calling for a slowdown is simpler than the grand narrative: they didn't invest in compute as aggressively as everyone else.

Look at **[OpenAI](https://openai.com)**. Look at Musk, who just handed them what amounts to a giant cluster on rent. Anthropic didn't move as fast on compute as the others did - and now they're the ones calling for everyone to ease off the gas.

It's a beautifully packaged message, wrapped in safety and responsibility. But strip away the eloquent gibberish and you're left with a much more mundane story: when you're behind on the hardware, "let's all slow down" starts to sound a lot more appealing.
