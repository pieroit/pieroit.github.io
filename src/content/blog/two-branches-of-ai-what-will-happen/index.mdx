They hand you a million euros and you have to decide how to invest it. You have two options - you have to split it across two different bets. And the bets are on artificial intelligence.

## Bet one: AI close to the person

The first is agents as browsers, as personal assistants, as something that generally lives on your computer as software - your digital twin, your assistant, your something, your twin - and it does things on your behalf and for you. This is the vision of [Claude Code](https://www.anthropic.com/claude-code), the vision of skills, the vision of the CLIs, the vision where the [MCP](https://modelcontextprotocol.io) protocol isn't needed. It's the vision of whoever sees artificial intelligence as something close to the person.

## Bet two: AI as a networked service

The other vision is that of intelligence as a service, as an endpoint, as something that sits on the network and that you call to have your requests fulfilled. So in the first case, the AI is a bit more like what a browser was - a navigator of data. In the second vision, the AI is closer to what a website was: something you reach that's exposed on the network.

So you've got a million in hand. And it's not allowed to say "half and half" - don't be wishy-washy about it or I'll get annoyed. Make a choice in life. So take a side, and above all tell me why. Where are you investing this million?
